Truth or Elaborate Lie

Only you can tell the difference.

Ever wonder which of your friends are honest? Maybe you question how often family members lie to you? Are you concerned with how often politicians manipulate you with their words? How do you know Facts from Alternative Facts? Well wonder no more! (Actually, you’re about to start wondering a LOT more.) This party game is here to shed some light on these questions and more!

Truth or Elaborate Lie is a parlor game where players make statments—truths, lies, or elaborate lies—and the other players guess whether it's the truth or an elaborate lie. It can be played in a room, on a river, or from a computer. Truth or Elaborate Lie will make you smarter and more adept at seeing and knowing the Truth vs Elaborate Lies. After all, only you can tell the difference. And that’s the truth…

Or an elaborate lie.

Authoritarian Rule

3 rounds
1 statement per person per round
1 point for a correct claim
0 points for a wrong claim
The person with the most points wins
1 Alternative Fact Check per game

How to Play


  • 1

    Get People

    Get a couple of frenemies (or complete strangers) together.

  • 2

    Make a Statement

    Someone starts by saying "Truth or Elaborate Lie:" and they make a statement.

  • 3

    Vote

    Individually, everyone votes whether they think the statement is a Truth or Elaborate Lie.

  • 4

    Verify

    The person making the statement claims it as Truth or Elaborate Lie.

  • 5

    Award Points

    Points are given to the guesstimating players: 1 point for a matching claim and 0 points for a different claim.

  • 6

    Everyone Else

    Each person makes a statement and the other players guesstimate (repeat Steps 2-5).

  • 7

    Total the Points

    Once everyone makes a statement, the round is over and the scores are tallied and reported.

  • 8

    Round 2

    Start the next round (repeat Steps 2-7).

  • 9

    Round 3

    Start the final round (repeat Steps 2-7).

  • 10

    Winner's Truth

    The person with the most points gets to ask any other player a question that MUST be answered truthfully.

  • B

    Bonus Rules

    Add any bonus rules to make the game more lively. For example, whenever a player earns a point, they can make another player take a drink.

How to Play


  • 1

    Get People

    Get a couple of frenemies (or complete strangers) together.

  • 2

    Make a Statement

    Someone starts by saying "Truth or Elaborate Lie:" and they make a statement.

  • 3

    Vote

    Everyone votes whether they think the statement is a Truth or Elaborate Lie—the statement maker goes last.

  • 4

    Tally the Votes

    The votes are tallied—ties are decided by the statement maker.

  • 5

    The Law

    The statement is branded as Truth or Elaborate Lie and becomes law.

  • 6

    A Rule

    The voters who supported the law get to make up a rule to accompany the law.

  • 7

    The Rule is Law

    Everyone must accept the new law and follow the new rule.

  • 8

    Cycle Thru

    A new person makes a statement (repeat Steps 2-6).

  • 9

    Breaking a Rule

    If a player breaks a rule, they are put in Contempt of Court: they are unable to participate for the rest of the round. They will remain silent, and their vote for the current round is nullified and ignored. (Note: Statement makers are still allowed to decide tie-breakers)

  • B

    Bonus Rules

    Add any bonus rules to make the game more lively. For example, whenever a rule is broken, that player takes a drink.

Alternative Facts

Dispute the claim on a statement.

Each player can Alternative Fact Check once per game.

If an Alternative Fact Check is initiated:

  1. Each player can reference 1 source to support or conflict the current statement and claim.
  2. Players decide how long they have to research. Majority consensus wins. If there's a tie, the Alternative Fact initiator decides the time limit.
  3. Players discuss all of the supporting sources.
  4. The group votes whether the original statement was the Truth or Elaborate Lie.
  5. In case of a tie, the claim remains the same.
  6. Players with matching claims get 1 point, including the statement maker. (Note: only applies in Authoritarian Rule)
